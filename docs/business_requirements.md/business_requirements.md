# Business Requirements Document (BRD) - E-Commerce Customer Retention Engine

## 1. The Retention Baseline
* **Question:** What percentage of our customers return to make a second purchase within 30, 60, and 90 days?
* **Why the Marketing Team Cares:** [The CMO needs to know the exact percentage of returning customers to calculate the payback period on ad spend and confirm that their marketing campaigns are driving long-term loyalty rather than brief spikes]

## 2. The Drop-off Milestone
* **Question:** In which month of the customer lifecycle does the steepest drop-off occur? (e.g., Is it Month 1 to Month 2, or Month 2 to Month 3?)
* **Why the Marketing Team Cares:** If the drop-off is steepest right after Month 1, the Head of CX checks for early post-purchase friction (like shipping updates or product quality), while the CMO adjusts re-engagement email workflows.
## 3. The Product Trigger
* **Question:** Do customers who buy from specific product categories (like Electronics) have better long-term retention than those who buy from other categories (like Apparel)?
* **Why the Marketing Team Cares:** The Head of CX monitors this to identify high-return or low-quality product categories that damage retention, while the VP of Sales flags cross-selling opportunities to maximize revenue.
## 4. The Value Analysis
* **Question:** Are our repeat customers buying cheaper items over time, or is their Average Order Value (AOV) increasing?
* **Why the Marketing Team Cares:** The VP of Sales is responsible for revenue velocity. If repeat customers are spending less per transaction (dropping Average Order Value), it indicates a pricing or engagement issue that requires structural adjustment.

## 5. Geographic Loyalty
* **Question:** Do certain states or cities exhibit significantly higher customer loyalty and repeat purchases than others?
* **Why the Marketing Team Cares:** The CMO targets regional ad spends in highly loyal clusters, while the VP of Sales collaborates with logistics partners to ensure optimal distribution center placement in those top-performing zones.
